St. John Bosco Parish - Tondo

Parish Overview

Don Bosco Parish in Tondo, Manila is a Catholic community guided by the Salesians of Don Bosco, dedicated to serving the spiritual and developmental needs of the youth and families in the area. The parish promotes faith formation, community service, and active participation in the sacraments, following the spirit of St. John Bosco’s mission to uplift the young, especially the poor.

Vision

Towards the totality and transformation of marginalized and out-of-school youth into good Christians, honest and productive citizens through education and spiritual and values formations.

Mission

  • Educate the youth academically according to the Department of Education and Don Bosco’s Preventive System.
  • Evangelize the youth through Christian and moral values formation.
  • Empower the youth to showcase talents, participate in civic and social activities, and become agents of social transformation.

Foundation of Salesians of Don Bosco

The Salesian Congregation was founded to continue Don Bosco’s work for abandoned boys and later for girls through the Daughters of Mary Help of Christians. It was formally recognized by Pope Pius IX in 1873. The congregation expanded internationally with missions, educational institutions, and lay organizations, carrying forward Don Bosco’s vision of holistic youth development.

Holy Mass Etiquette

  • Come early – Arrive 15 minutes before Mass.
  • Dress properly – Show reverence to God.
  • Phones off – Silence mobile devices.
  • Genuflect – Kneel on right knee before entering pew.
  • Participate – Respond, sing, and listen attentively.
  • Commune – Examine yourself before receiving Holy Communion.
  • Think of others – Avoid loud talking or unnecessary movement.
  • Be still – Stay until the final blessing.
